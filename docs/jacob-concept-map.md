## JC001 — "Music in 5 Levels of Difficulty: HARMONY" ft. Herbie Hancock (WIRED, 15:41, 2018)
🔗 https://youtu.be/eRkgK4jfi6M · Topic: **Harmony**, explained at 5 levels (child → teen → student → professional → legend).
The single tune **"Amazing Grace"** is reharmonized at every level — a perfect spine for a whole module.

### The 5 levels (with timestamps)
- **L1 · Child [0:19]** — Harmony = voices sounding nice together. The big idea a 6-year-old can hold: *you can **decide how a melody feels**, and more notes = more emotion.* The Amazing Grace melody alone is "lonely — no one knows how it feels."
- **L2 · Teen [1:06]** — The **triad** (3-part harmony); **major = happy, minor = dark/spooky** (feelings, not labels). Harmony as a journey: *"leave home, return home, and learn something along the way."* The harmonizer's job = **find the narrative** and make it make sense (start Amazing Grace on an F that isn't home → "where am I? get back home").
- **L3 · Student (guitarist) [2:43]** — Harmony is a **language**: more words = more you can say. The **Circle of Fifths** has a **bright side and a dark side**; "visit B♭, the sun comes out," add color notes (F, then C, then G). Plays Amazing Grace plain in F, then a lush reharm: F maj7/A → B♭maj7(add A) → Fmaj7(add e,d,g) → Dm7(add g) → C7sus→min → B♭maj → G7 → F♯maj7.
- **L4 · Professional [4:49]** — The **harmonic (overtone) series** *and its mirror, the **undertone series*** ("branches grow up, roots grow down; the key center is the floor"). Reharm isn't only *adding* notes — **inversions** rearrange what you already have. The discovery: **every melody note works with every bass note** (demo over F: F maj, F min, G♭maj7, G♭dim, G♭min-maj7, Gm7, Gsus, G7, G7♯11, G7♭13, A♭sus, A♭maj7, A♭maj7♯5♯11). With everything possible, the skill is **knowing what you want to say emotionally**. **Negative harmony** = the polarity between under- and overtone series (a minor plagal cadence is "so moving"). **Context:** arrive in F major *from D♭* and "the sun comes out"; arrive *from A major* and "the sun goes in" — **where you come FROM changes the feeling.**
- **L5 · Herbie Hancock [8:28]** — Courage to choose comes from **life experience**; "music is not different from life." The "**don't follow the crowd**" chord (a maj7♯-type chord that *functions* as a dominant without being one). C7 and A♭7 are secretly **related** (tritone-sub thinking) — voice it right and "you get away with it." An **arrival chord sharing the bass note** of the chord before → "inevitability… I was there all along." And the legendary **"butter notes"** story: Miles Davis told Herbie *"don't play the **bottom** notes,"* Herbie heard *"butter"* — started **leaving out the obvious notes (the 3rd and 7th)** → changed his voicings forever.

### Concepts → where each deepens our app → on the lever harp
| Jacob's idea | Deepens (our chapter/tool) | On the lever harp |
|---|---|---|
| Major/minor & chords as **feelings/colors** | Modes "character/mood" model; Ch3 | Our character-note idea, extended — name the feeling first, find the chord second |
| **Triad**, leave-&-return-home, the narrative | Ch6 Triads, Ch7 Cadences | Set the key with levers, then tell the home→away→home story with rolled chords |
| **Circle of Fifths** bright/dark + adding color | **Circle of Fifths tool**, Ch2, Ch9 | The bright/dark arc is already our reachable-keys arc; "visit a key" = a lever flip |
| **Reharmonization / color chords** | Ch17 secondary dominants, Ch19 mode mixture, Ch8 sevenths | Which reharm chords are lever-reachable; choreograph the lever flips |
| **Harmonic series + UNDERTONE series** (mirror) | Ch38 (Science of Sound) — *extend with the undertone/mirror* | Your strings give the overtone side; the undertone is the conceptual "roots" |
| **Inversions** before adding notes | Ch6 (inversions) | Inversions keep the hand in one string region — smooth + idiomatic |
| **Every melody note over every bass note** | Ch16 figured bass; left-hand/bass teaching | *Brilliant harp drill:* hold one low bass string ringing, roll every chord shape above it |
| **Negative harmony** (under/overtone polarity) | NEW — deepens chromatic harmony | Build the **Negative-Harmony Mirror** toy; flag which mirrored chords are reachable |

## JC002 — "Super-Ultra-Hyper-Mega-Meta Lydian" (fan analysis incl. a Jacob clip, 10:37)
🔗 https://youtu.be/MLunr5WL0kc · ⚠️ This is a **third-party theory breakdown**, not Jacob's own channel; it embeds a short Jacob clip (≈0:44–1:46). We teach the *concept* and cite Jacob's words; treat the rest as commentary.

### The concept
- **Jacob's own framing [~0:44]:** Super-Ultra-Hyper-Mega-Meta Lydian is **"not a scale — a sound."** It's **Lydian that doesn't stop at the root** when you arrive — you keep climbing by the Lydian pattern, **continuously wrapping around the bright (sharp) side of the circle of fifths**. To him that's *"Lydian at its most comfortable — optimum brightness."* Go the other way and you wrap around the **dark (flat) side** — the opposite sound. This shaped how he thinks about **navigation and "colouring in."**
- **Why "not a scale":** if you actually completed it, it would span **~7 octaves** (beyond hearing) before repeating — so it's a directional *sound/colour*, not a contained octave scale.
- **The duality (very Jacob):** **fifths brighten (major-ward), fourths darken (minor-ward).** SUHMM-Lydian climbs by fifths (brightening); its dark mirror climbs by fourths.

### → where it deepens our app → on the lever harp
| Idea | Deepens | On the lever harp |
|---|---|---|
| Brightness as a **continuous axis** (sharp-ward = brighter) | **Circle of Fifths tool**, our bright/dark arc; Modes brightness order | Brightness = how many levers you've raised; the sharp-side keys are literally "brighter" |
| **Lydian = brightest** (the raised 4th) | Modes (Lydian character note); Ch41 | Lydian on harp = raise the 4th; the floating ♯4 is the brightness magnet |
| **Fifths-brighten / fourths-darken** duality | Ch9 (circle progression), Negative-Harmony module | Root motion by fifths vs fourths = different lever regions & feelings |
| "A sound, not a scale" (direction over container) | Framing for the whole "colour" approach | Teach colour/direction over fixed scales — fits harp's set-then-explore nature |

**Note for the section:** SUHMM is a perfect "wow" concept but is largely *conceptual* on a diatonic lever harp (you can't climb 7 octaves of ♯4s). We teach the **brightness-direction idea** and tie it to the Circle of Fifths + Lydian, candidly.
